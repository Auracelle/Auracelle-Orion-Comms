## Overview

Auracelle Orion is a domain-partitioned cyber governance simulation platform that scores governance performance, traces failure to its source, and proves the value of policy investments — before a real crisis forces the question.

Most cyber wargaming tools collapse physical infrastructure and software supply chains into a single risk score, producing results too generic for institutional use. Orion separates them, scores them independently, and models precisely how failure travels between them.

---

## Platform Architecture

Orion operates across three architectural domains:

| Domain | Code | Scope |
|--------|------|-------|
| Infrastructure Domain | IFD | Physical plant, OT/ICS/SCADA, network hardware, power and cooling systems |
| Software Domain | SWD | Application stacks, firmware, cloud-native services, CI/CD pipelines, dependency graphs |
| Supply-Chain Trust Layer | SCTL | Directed trust graph connecting vendors, integrators, and operators across both domains |

---

## Core Capabilities

- **Quantified Governance Scoring** — Every scenario produces a traceable score decomposable to its source across both domains
- **Counterfactual Policy Analysis** — Re-run any scenario with a different configuration to measure the governance value of specific investments
- **Accelerated Decision-Making** — Domain-partitioned time compression quantifies decision quality under realistic incident pressure
- **Live Data Integration** — Real-time grounding via World Bank, SIPRI, UN Comtrade, and US Export Controls APIs

## About

Auracelle Orion is an independent research initiative developed by Auracelle AI Governance Labs. Published frameworks from NATO, UN agencies, OECD, WEF, and NIST informed scenario design and regulatory mapping. Doctoral research at Bath Spa University provided academic grounding.

The platform represents independent synthesis of authoritative governance literature and over 20 years of practitioner experience across defense, intelligence, and critical infrastructure organizations including CIA, DHS, Army Intelligence and Security Command, San Francisco International Airport, and Maryland Department of Transportation.
